I'm a software developer and writer from New York City. Here are some of the things people tell me I'm good at.

Advanced full stack web development for clients such as PowerBar (Drupal), Center for Disease Control (Drupal, Solr) and Wellformative (Drupal, Drupal Commerce). I'm currently obsessed with Node.js, Angular.js and the MEAN stack, which I believe points the way to the future of web development. I've been watching the field evolve for a long time: my major past projects include Words Without Borders and (going way back) Time Warner's Pathfinder, where I learned my craft.
I specialize in Content Management Systems and Custom Search Engine Development are two of my specialties. I developed a custom back-end for Market Research Corporation's private repository of PDF documents, worked on a massively scaleable Autonomy-based legal search engine for Inference Data and also used Autonomy to power a new search engine for History Channel. I am currently very excited about the possibilities of MongoDB and Keystone CMS, a Node-based CMS platform which you can see in action right here on MarcEliotStein.com.
I love to see projects I work on succeed, and have often delved deep into Social Media Strategy to help my clients. I launched Foreign Policy magazine's Twitter presence, and helped the Department of Labor-funded project Virtual Career Network come up with a UX strategy that attracts audiences. I did early work in this area for iVillage, where I collaborated with the community team on a member profile redesign that brought in millions of pageviews a month and demonstrated the considerable power of organic social marketing.
Let's be honest: clients and companies hire me for my tech skills. But I also have a good eye for Art Design, and am proudest of projects on which I've had complete ownership of the visual experience and got to play with Photoshop as well as Linux. I favor Bootstrap for responsive design, enjoy working with advanced LESS and CSS, and always try to stay on top of the ever-changing field of UX. You can get an idea of my web design aesthetic by checking out The Law Firm of Laurence P. Greenberg, Practical Handbook of Group Counseling and Eli Stein Cartoons.
And what are we doing all this hard work for, if not to create a better world? I am deeply committed to causes like pacifism, environmental awareness and social justice, and have recently kicked off a crowdfunded project called Pacifism for the 21st Century, a work in progress.
Last but not least: what I'm really all about is Music, Literature & Art. I was the sole coder for Bob Dylan's first website back in 1996, and relaunched his website in 2001. I also built an innovative official website for Pearl Jam in 2006. The most enduring project of my life (other than parenthood) has been my own literary blog, Literary Kicks, which has been online (and morphing and changing with the times) since the summer of 1994.

Thanks for checking out the work I've done. If you'd like to work with me, please get in touch. By the way, the banner image at the top of the page is a rebus. If you're not sure what that means, or need a clue to figure it out, send me a note and I'll happily explain.
